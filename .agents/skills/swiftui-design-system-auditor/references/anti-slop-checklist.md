# Anti-Slop Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing redesigns, visual polish, screenshots, or broad UI direction. Be blunt about patterns that make an Apple app feel generic, web-first, or harder to use.

## Reject

- Generic purple or blue gradients.
- Too many same-looking cards.
- Random glass panels.
- Fake dashboard statistics.
- Decorative chips or badges without function.
- Identical text hierarchy everywhere.
- Weak primary action.
- Web SaaS layout forced into iPadOS.
- Excessive blur, glow, or shadows.
- Icon-only controls without clear purpose.
- Empty space that does not improve scanning.
- "Premium" polish that hurts reading, writing, drawing, or studying.

## Require

- One clear screen job.
- One dominant content area.
- One primary action.
- Stable reading and writing surfaces.
- Compact control chrome.
- Consistent spacing rhythm.
- System typography unless there is a deliberate product reason.
- Screenshot-backed critique when possible.
- Concrete SwiftUI changes, not mood words.
